Energy Act 2019

LEGISLATION

Kenya's primary energy sector legislation, assented on 12 March 2019. Establishes EPRA, REREC, NuPEA, and the Energy and Petroleum Tribunal, and governs licensing for electricity generation, transmission, distribution, renewable energy feed-in tariffs, geothermal resources, and downstream coal.

Petroleum Act Cap 308

LEGISLATION

Governs upstream petroleum exploration contracts, production sharing (Cost Oil/Profit Oil via R-Factor), midstream and downstream licensing, local content and training obligations, environmental compliance, decommissioning, and revenue sharing between the Government and contractors.

Green Hydrogen Strategy 2023

POLICY

Kenya's strategy for leveraging its abundant geothermal, wind, solar, and hydro resources for large-scale green hydrogen production. Developed with EU and GIZ support, it identifies four focus areas: strategy formulation, regulatory frameworks, financial mechanisms, and stakeholder engagement.

Kenya Energy Transition & Investment Plan 2023–2050 (KETIP)

STRATEGY

Modelled pathway for Kenya to reach Net Zero by 2050, requiring ~USD 600 billion in cumulative capital investment. Identifies six key decarbonization technologies — renewables, electrification, low-carbon hydrogen, battery EVs, clean cooking, and carbon capture — across power, transport, industry, and buildings.

KNEECS Implementation Plan 2022

EFFICIENCY

Detailed action plan implementing Kenya's National Energy Efficiency and Conservation Strategy across six sectors: households (MEPS for appliances and cookstoves), buildings (green building codes), manufacturing and agriculture (energy audits), transport (fuel economy, e-mobility), power supply (grid stability), and cross-cutting institutional capacity.

Ministry of Energy & Petroleum — Strategic Plan 2023–2027

ROADMAP

The Ministry's fifth-generation strategic plan, built around four Key Result Areas: energy resource development, access to energy products, environmental sustainability, and sector enablement. Reports installed generation capacity grew 40.9% to 3,312 MW (93%+ renewable) and grid connectivity reached 9.21 million customers by 2023.

IEA Kenya 2024 Policy Review

REPORT

International Energy Agency (IEA) comprehensive review of Kenya's energy policies, power market dynamics, regional power trade, clean energy finance metrics, and pathways to universal energy access.
